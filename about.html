About Me

Tcaci Dumitru

Final Year · Finance & Banking · West University of Timișoara

I'm a final-year Finance and Banking student at the Faculty of Economics and Business Administration, West University of Timișoara. My academic focus sits at the intersection of monetary policy, commercial banking, and capital markets — with a growing interest in the regulatory architecture that underpins modern financial systems.

I started this blog to think out loud about the things I'm studying, reading, and observing in the financial world — and to practice translating technical ideas into clear prose.

Growing up in Cluj-Napoca, I was surrounded by a family that talked about money the way other families talked about sports: constantly, passionately, and with strong opinions about everything. My father ran a small construction business, which meant I witnessed firsthand how credit cycles, interest rate movements, and currency fluctuations could affect a real enterprise — not just a textbook balance sheet. That grounding shaped the way I approach finance: always looking for the connection between abstract models and concrete human outcomes.

At university I've pursued a deliberately broad curriculum: corporate finance, investment analysis, banking regulation, international economics, and quantitative methods. I believe that specialization without breadth creates fragile expertise — the financial crises we study in economic history were almost always failures of narrow specialists who couldn't see the systemic picture.

Outside of coursework, I serve as vice-president of the university's Finance & Investment Club, where we run a mock portfolio competition each semester. Managing a simulated portfolio under real market conditions — with real peer pressure and public accountability — has taught me more about behavioral finance than any textbook on the subject.

I'm currently writing my bachelor's thesis on the transmission of ECB monetary policy decisions through the Romanian banking sector, with a particular focus on retail mortgage rates and small business credit availability. It's a topic I find genuinely fascinating, and I hope some of that fascination comes through in my writing here.
