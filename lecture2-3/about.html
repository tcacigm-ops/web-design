Background

Growing up in Chișinău, Moldova gave me an early and unfiltered education in economic reality. Moldova sits at the intersection of Eastern European transition economics, currency volatility, and the everyday pressures of a developing market — the kind of context that makes abstract concepts like inflation, capital flight, and banking fragility feel very concrete, very fast. That background shapes how I read financial news and why I'm drawn to questions about monetary stability and institutional trust.

Choosing to study Finance and Banking at West University of Timișoara was a deliberate step westward — not just geographically, but professionally. Romania's integration into EU financial markets, its evolving regulatory landscape, and its proximity to major banking groups from Austria, France, and Italy made Timișoara an ideal place to understand how European finance actually operates at a regional level.

Outside of my coursework, I work as an operator and consultant for online education businesses. My role involves growth strategy, funnel optimization, and the financial mechanics of scaling subscription and cohort-based models. It's taught me things no classroom covers: how unit economics drive decisions, how cash flow constraints shape strategy, and how founders think differently from analysts. I bring that operational lens into how I read corporate finance and banking — always asking what the numbers mean for the people running the business, not just the people modeling it.

Travel is the other constant in my life. Moving between Chișinău, Timișoara, and wherever work takes me has made me comfortable with ambiguity and genuinely curious about how different economies and financial cultures work on the ground. There's no better reminder that finance is a human institution than watching how differently people in different countries relate to banks, credit, and money.
